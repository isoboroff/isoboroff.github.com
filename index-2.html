    I'm in love with a pod    Posted: 2009-09-02 09:48

    Here's the blog post circulating from BackBlaze on their 67TB storage pod:
     
    http://blog.backblaze.com/2009/09/01/petabytes-on-a-budget-how-to-build-cheap-cloud-storage/
     
    I am in love.  Well, somewhat.  I've spent a chunk of the past year
    spec'ing a storage cluster so that the research group I work with can
    continue to scale to the data need of the information retrieval
    research community.  (See http://boston.lti.cs.cmu.edu/Data/clueweb09/
    for what I have to work with today, and I'd like to buy something that
    lasts for two or three years.)

    My favorite graphic in the blog post shows dollars per petabyte from various vendors. 

    I spent a lot of time collecting similar numbers. The cluster I ended
    up specifying is similar but uses a bunch of cluster compute nodes
    each of which can host 5-10TB each. That's because I need CPU cycles
    near the data; BackBlaze is just trying to solve a data problem.
     
    I love their solution to the data problem. NetApp sells a similar box
    but I don't have a quote on what it costs. Anyone know?
     
    They are sidestepping a real problem, which is that Linux just doesn't
    do tens of terabytes all together very well. My cluster is just a
    Hadoop platform, but this box is no good for Hadoop, it has too many
    disks in one place. Well, love isn't about perfection, it's about
    reveling in the flaws, right?
     
    And I want my boxes in red, too. I haven't been able to get boxes in
    nice colors since SGI.
